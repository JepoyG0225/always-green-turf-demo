The Installation Process

7 Phases. Every Time.
No Shortcuts, No Exceptions.

This is the process we use on every single installation — refined across 500+ Arizona yards. Nothing is skipped. Nothing is rushed.

01
1Phase 1

Phase 1: Demo & Full Site Prep

We start by completely clearing your existing yard — removing all vegetation, old ground covers, and surface materials down to bare soil. Our team grades the site to the correct slope for drainage, and carefully exposes any underground infrastructure.

02
2Phase 2

Phase 2: Compacted DG Sub-Base

This is where most budget installers cut corners — and where your lawn's longevity is decided. We spread 2–3 inches of crushed decomposed granite and compact it with a plate compactor, creating a firm, level, drainage-optimized base built specifically for Arizona's caliche and sandy soils.

03
3Phase 3

Phase 3: Turf Layout & Relaxation

Your premium synthetic grass is rolled out and allowed to expand naturally in the Arizona sun for 30–60 minutes. This critical step relaxes the fibers and ensures the turf lays perfectly flat. We position it with careful attention to blade direction and stitch patterns for a seamless, natural appearance.

04
4Phase 4

Phase 4: Precision Cutting & Invisible Seams

Our installers precision-cut the turf to fit your exact yard shape — any perimeter, curve, or pattern. We intentionally overcut edges by one inch along borders to allow for clean tucking. Where multiple turf sections meet, we create tight, invisible seams that disappear completely once installation is complete.

05
5Phase 5

Phase 5: Full Perimeter Securing

With the turf properly positioned and cut, we secure the entire perimeter using non-galvanized turf nails or staples placed approximately six inches apart. All edges are carefully tucked and folded for a clean, professional finish. Every border is double-checked for tightness and a natural look.

06
6Phase 6

Phase 6: Infill Application & Power Brushing

Before infill is applied, a power brush raises all turf blades to an upright position. Infill material is then evenly distributed throughout the turf. The infill acts as ballast to hold the turf firmly against the sub-base, and keeps the individual grass blades standing upright for that realistic, lush appearance.

07
7Phase 7

Phase 7: Final Grooming, Rinse & Walkthrough

This is the phase most installers rush. We don't. We power-brush the entire surface twice, rinse every inch of the lawn, and walk the finished project with you side by side — pointing out every detail before we consider the job done. We don't leave until you're satisfied.

The Installation Process

Everything you need to know before we arrive at your Phoenix-area home.

Most residential installations in the Phoenix metro area are completed in a single day. Larger projects — full backyards over 2,000 sq ft, multi-zone installs, or commercial properties — may take two days. We'll give you a precise timeline during your free on-site estimate.
You don't need to be home throughout the day, but we ask that someone is available at the start to walk through the site with us, and at the end for the final inspection walkthrough. This ensures the completed project matches your vision exactly before we leave.
During Phase 1, we carefully expose and map all underground irrigation lines. Existing irrigation is typically capped off or removed since artificial turf requires no watering. If you have trees, garden beds, or other planted areas nearby that still need irrigation, we'll work around those systems to preserve them.
Arizona's caliche layer is notoriously hard and can block drainage. Our Phase 2 sub-base system addresses this directly — we break through the caliche where necessary and install a properly graded decomposed granite base that channels water away efficiently, even during monsoon storms. Proper drainage is non-negotiable in our installs.
Absolutely. Our Phase 4 custom fitting step is designed specifically for complex yards. We precision-cut turf to fit around pool decks, tree root zones, paver borders, raised planters, and any other landscaping features. The result looks intentional, clean, and completely natural.
Synthetic turf can heat up in direct summer sun — it's a common question for Arizona homeowners. We address this through our infill selection. We offer heat-reduction infill options including coated sand and organic infills that significantly reduce surface temperature. Many homeowners also mist the turf briefly before outdoor activities, which drops the temperature quickly. We'll recommend the right infill for your specific sun exposure during the estimate.
